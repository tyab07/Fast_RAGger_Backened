CFD Campus – Transportation Rules & Guidelines for Students
1. RFID / Transport Card Requirement
•	All students must carry their RFID Transport Card or Bus Card while traveling in the university bus.
•	Registered students will be issued a seat number and bus card by the Transport Office.
________________________________________
2. Travel Eligibility & Fines
•	No student is allowed to travel without registration or without a valid bus ticket.
•	Travelling without registration or ticket will result in a Rs. 1,000 fine.
•	Non-registered students may travel by paying:
o	Rs. 200 for one-way
o	Rs. 300 for both sides
________________________________________
3. Bus Timings & Punctuality
•	Buses do not wait for late students.
•	Students must be present at their stop at least 5 minutes before the scheduled arrival time.
•	Students must be seated in the bus at least 2 minutes before departure from campus to avoid injuries.
________________________________________
4. Missed Bus Policy
•	If a student misses their designated bus, they may use another bus if space is available.
________________________________________
5. Route Usage & Route Change
•	Students must travel only on the bus route they are registered for.
•	A route change can be requested only with a valid reason and Transport Office approval.
________________________________________
6. Bus Pickup Policy
•	The bus will pick students only from their allotted stops.
•	The “first-come, first-served” rule applies for seat allocation during registration.
________________________________________
7. Discipline Inside the Bus
Students must maintain proper discipline. The following are strictly prohibited:
•	Hooting, shouting, or loud discussions
•	Playing music
•	Political conversations or arguments
•	Any form of disturbance affecting others
________________________________________
8. No Sports or Gaming Activities
The following activities are not allowed inside the bus:
•	Playing cards
•	Ludo
•	Antakshari
•	Any sports or entertainment games
________________________________________
9. Bus Maintenance & Alternative Arrangements
•	If a bus becomes faulty or breaks down, the Transport Office will arrange an alternative bus for students.
________________________________________
10. Shuttle Service
•	Shuttle service is provided to registered students on:
o	Fridays
o	Examination days
•	For events, shuttle service will be provided only if approved by the Event Committee.
________________________________________
11. Seating Arrangement
•	The front rows are reserved for girls.
•	Boys’ seating starts after the girls’ section ends.
________________________________________
12. Bus Attendant & Security
•	Every bus has a bus attendant responsible for monitoring student behavior and ensuring safety.
13. Registration Fee & Policy
•	The transport registration fee is Rs. 35,000 per semester.
•	No installments are allowed; the full amount must be paid at once.
•	After the due date, the fee becomes:
o	Rs. 35,000 + Rs. 5,000 late fine
14.Registration Notification
•	Students will be informed about transport registration through email.
•	The email will include details about required documents, payment method, and submission steps.
15.Bus Departure Timings
•	Regular buses depart at 5:00 PM sharp.
•	Shuttle buses depart at 2:00 PM.
•	Students must ensure they are inside the bus before departure time.



✅ Transport FAQs (Answers Added)
1. Does the university provide transport services?
Yes, the university provides transport services to registered students.
2. What areas does the transport cover?
Transport covers major areas of the city, following fixed, predefined routes issued by the Transport Office.
3. How many buses operate on each route?
Each route has one designated bus, and additional buses are arranged only when needed.
4. What are the morning pick-up timings?
Morning pick-up timings vary by route but usually fall between 6:55 AM to 7:55 AM, depending on location.
5. What are the drop-off timings after classes?
All regular buses depart from campus at 5:00 PM sharp.
6. Is transport available on all weekdays?
Yes, transport services operate on all regular university working days.
7. Are there weekend or makeup-class buses?
No, buses do not run on weekends unless specially announced by the Transport Office.
8. How long is the travel time from my area?
Travel time depends on your location but generally ranges between 20 to 60 minutes.
9. Where is the nearest stop to my home?
You can check the route list or contact the transport office to find the nearest designated stop.
10. Are the bus routes fixed?
Yes, routes are fixed and followed daily.
11. How often do routes change?
Routes are rarely changed. Any changes are communicated to students through email.
12. Can I request a new bus stop?
You may submit a request, but approval depends on feasibility and Transport Office decision.
13. Can the bus stop closer to my home?
No, buses only stop at the official designated stops.
14. How do I check the exact pick-up time?
Pick-up timings are provided in the bus route document and may also be announced via email.
15. Will the bus wait for late students?
No, the bus does not wait for late students.
16. What happens if I miss the bus?
If you miss the bus, it is your responsibility to reach campus on your own.
17. Can I take a different bus if I miss mine?
Yes, you may take another bus only if there is available space.
18. Are there GPS or tracking options for buses?
No GPS tracking system is currently available.
19. Who do I contact if the bus is late?
You may contact your route supervisor or Transport Office through their provided phone numbers.
20. How do I apply for transport?
You will receive an email notification with requirements and instructions for registration.
21. Is there a separate transport form?
Yes, transport requires filling out a separate registration form.
22. Do I have to renew registration every semester?
Yes, transport registration is required every semester.
23. How do I know which bus I’m assigned to?
Your assigned route and bus number will be provided after registration.
24. Will I get a transport ID or card?
Yes, a Transport/Bus Card (RFID card) is issued to every registered student.
25. Can I change my bus later?
Yes, but only with valid reasons and approval from the Transport Office.
26. Can I change my stop during the semester?
Yes, stop change requests can be made with a valid reason.
27. Can I cancel transport anytime?
Yes, you can cancel transport.
Refund depends on how much of the semester has passed.
28. What is the monthly transport fee?
Transport fee is not monthly.
It is Rs. 35,000 per semester (full payment, no installments).
29. Is the fee fixed for all routes?
Yes, the transport fee is the same for all students regardless of route.
30. Is the transport fee refundable?
Yes, but only proportionally, based on usage time before cancellation.
31. How do I pay the transport fee?
Fee is paid through the payment method mentioned in the registration email.
32. Do I have to pay during vacation months?
If you repeat or improve subjects during summer and use university transport, then you must pay a transport fee of Rs. 17,000 for the summer period.
If you do not come to university during summer, then no transport charges apply.
33. Are there fines for late fee payment?
Yes, the late fee is:
Rs. 35,000 + Rs. 5,000 fine after the due date.
34. Are there separate buses for girls?
No, but front rows are reserved for girls.
35. Is there reserved seating for female students?
Yes, the first rows of every bus are strictly reserved for female students.
36. Are seats fixed or free to choose?
Seats are assigned based on first-come-first-serve at registration, and students must use their allotted seat number.
37. What if the bus is full?
Transport registration works on a first-come, first-served basis.
Once all seats in a bus are filled, registration for that bus automatically closes.
Students are then offered the option to register in another bus that passes near their area.
38. Are students allowed to stand?
No, standing in the bus is not allowed for safety reasons.
39. What are the safety rules inside the bus?
•	No shouting or loud discussions
•	No music or political arguments
•	No sports/games (cards, Ludo, etc.)
•	Follow the attendant’s instructions
40. Is there a transport in-charge I can contact?
Yes, every route has a transport supervisor, and the Transport Office also provides contact numbers.
41. What happens if the bus breaks down?
The Transport Office will arrange an alternative/breakdown bus.
42. Will a backup bus be sent?
Yes, a backup bus is sent in case of breakdown.
43. Do buses operate during heavy rain?
Yes, buses operate normally unless the university officially closes.
44. Do buses operate during exams?
Yes, transport is available during exams.
Shuttle services also operate at 2:00 PM.
45. Are the drivers trained and verified?
Yes, all drivers are trained and officially verified by the university.
46. What should I do if I face an issue with the driver?
Report immediately to the Transport Office or bus supervisor.
47. Are first-year students given any route priority?
No, all students follow the same registration procedure.
48. Can I bring large bags or project items on the bus?
Yes, if they do not block aisles or disturb other passengers.
49. Is transport available for evening students?
Only if the university announces evening transport for specific programs or exams.
50. Can I take transport one-way only (morning or evening)?
Yes, non-registered students can pay Rs. 200 (one-way) or Rs. 300 (round trip) per day.
